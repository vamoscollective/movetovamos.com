About Vamos Collective

Built for the moments when university learning feels harder than it should.

Vamos Collective began with a simple observation: while academic support is commonplace in secondary school and junior college, students are often left to navigate university largely on their own.

Our Story

Built from a frustration we knew too well.

University can be a strange experience.

For many students, the transition from JC or polytechnic to university is far steeper than expected. The content becomes more specialised, the workload increases, and the guidance that students were once used to often disappears.

You're expected to figure things out independently. For some students, that comes naturally. For others, it doesn't.

And that's okay.

The Idea

How it started

The founders of Vamos Collective come from different faculties in NUS and became friends through a student organisation.

While one had long dreamed of building something in the education and tutoring space, the other arrived at the idea through personal experience.

Having performed well enough academically to enter a competitive university course, one of us assumed that university would simply be a continuation of what had worked before.

It wasn't.

The Challenge

Learning independence the hard way

University learning operates very differently from what most students are used to.

Lectures introduce complex ideas quickly. Reading lists seem endless. Professors are researchers and academics, not classroom teachers in the traditional sense.

Students are expected to chart their own path through unfamiliar material, often with limited personalised guidance.

For some, that transition is smooth. For many others, it isn't.

The Realisation

Sometimes the best guidance comes from someone who has recently been there.

During difficult semesters, help came from peers and seniors who had already walked the same path.

They shared study strategies, reading approaches, exam techniques, and practical advice that couldn't easily be found in lecture notes or textbooks.

But there was only so much time that could reasonably be asked of friends and classmates before it began to feel one-sided.

That raised a simple question:

Why is tuition so common before university, yet so difficult to find once students arrive there?

The more we looked, the more we realised that this wasn't a personal problem. It was a common one.

Why Vamos Exists

Independence shouldn't mean isolation.

We believe that needing help is normal.

University is challenging by design. Students are expected to think critically, learn independently, and adapt quickly to unfamiliar material.

But independence should never be confused with struggling alone.

Sometimes you don't need another lecture recording, another textbook, or another AI-generated summary.

Sometimes you simply need someone who has successfully navigated the same module, course, or academic challenge before you.

That's why we created Vamos Collective.

We connect students with carefully screened peer tutors and senior mentors who combine strong academic performance with the ability to teach clearly and effectively.

Because being knowledgeable is important.

Being able to help someone else learn is what truly matters.

Ready to Get Started?

You don't have to figure it all out alone.

Whether you're struggling with a difficult module, searching for better study strategies, or simply looking for guidance from someone who has been there before, we're here to help.
