About Vamos Collective

Built from a frustration we knew too well.

University can be a strange experience.

For many students, the transition from JC or polytechnic to university is far steeper than expected. The content becomes more specialised, the workload increases, and the guidance that students were once used to often disappears.

You're expected to figure things out independently. For some students, that comes naturally. For others, it doesn't.

And that's okay.

The idea

Vamos Collective began with a simple observation.

The founders came from different faculties in NUS and became friends through a student organisation. While one had long been interested in the education and tutoring space, the other arrived at the idea through personal experience.

Having done well enough academically to enter a competitive university course, she assumed that university would simply be a continuation of what had worked before.

It wasn't.

The realisation

The first year was difficult. The content was unfamiliar. The expectations were different. Lectures often raised more questions than answers.

Like many university students, she struggled to understand not just what to study, but how to study.

Over time, help came from peers and seniors who had already walked the same path. They shared notes, study methods, exam strategies, and insights that couldn't easily be found in a textbook.

But there was only so much time that could reasonably be asked of friends.

Eventually, a simple question emerged: why is tuition so common in secondary school and junior college, but so difficult to find at the university level?

Why Vamos Exists

We believe that needing help is normal.

University learning is independent by design, but independence shouldn't mean isolation.

Sometimes you don't need another textbook, another lecture recording, or another AI-generated explanation.

Sometimes you just need someone who has successfully navigated the same course, module, or exam before you.

That's why we created Vamos Collective.

We connect students with carefully screened peer tutors and senior mentors who combine strong academic performance with the ability to teach clearly and effectively. Because being knowledgeable is important. Being able to help someone else learn is what truly matters.

You don't have to figure it all out alone.

Whether you're struggling with a difficult module, searching for better study strategies, or simply looking for guidance from someone who has been there before, we're here to help.
